Neapolitan Pizza Recipe

Description

Hailing from Naples, Italy, Neapolitan pizza is known for its simplicity and reliance on fresh, top-notch ingredients. The star is the thin, chewy crust with a hint of char, cooked to perfection in a blazing wood-fired oven. Classic toppings are equally uncomplicated, featuring just tomato sauce, fresh mozzarella, and fragrant basil for the Margherita pizza, or a simple tomato, garlic, and oregano combination for the Marinara. With its focus on quality and tradition, Neapolitan pizza is a culinary experience that celebrates fresh flavors and age-old techniques

Ingredients

For a classic Neapolitan pizza, you only need a few key ingredients:
  • Dough: This typically uses high-gluten flour like "00" flour, water, yeast, and salt.
  • Tomatoes: San Marzano tomatoes are preferred for their sweetness and acidity.
  • Cheese: Fresh mozzarella is the hero, sliced or torn into small pieces.
  • Fresh basil: Adds pops of fresh flavor and aroma.
  • Extra virgin olive oil: Drizzled on top for richness.
That's it! Neapolitan pizza relies on the quality of these simple ingredients to create a delicious and iconic pizza.

Steps

  1. Dough:

    • In a large bowl, whisk together 2 cups (240g) "00" flour (or bread flour), 1/8 tsp instant yeast, and 1/2 tsp granulated sugar.
    • Gradually add 3/4 cup (170g) lukewarm water (105°F to 115°F), mixing with a dough whisk or your hands until a shaggy dough forms.
    • Add 1 tbsp olive oil and 1 1/4 tsp (8g) table salt, knead for 10 minutes on a lightly floured surface.
    • Form the dough into a ball, place in a lightly oiled bowl, cover, and let rise at room temperature for 2 hours. Refrigerate for at least 8 hours, ideally overnight.

  2. Sauce:

    • Combine 1 cup crushed San Marzano tomatoes (or high-quality canned tomatoes), 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil, 1 tsp dried oregano, and a pinch of red pepper flakes (optional) in a small bowl. Season with salt to taste.

  3. Assemble:

    • Lightly flour a surface. Stretch dough into a 12-inch round.
    • Spread a thin layer of sauce over the dough, leaving a 1-inch border.
    • Distribute 8 oz fresh mozzarella cheese, sliced thin, evenly over the sauce.
    • Drizzle with a little extra virgin olive oil.

  4. Bake:

    • Bake on a preheated pizza stone (ideally 450-500°F) for 6-8 minutes, or until golden brown and bubbly with charring.
    • Alternatively, bake on a preheated baking sheet for a slightly less crispy crust.

  5. Serve:

    • Garnish with fresh basil leaves and sea salt flakes. Slice and enjoy immediately!
