Kolkata Style Chicken Biryani

Description

Kolkata-style chicken biryani is a fragrant rice dish layered with succulent chicken pieces, fluffy potatoes, and often, hard-boiled eggs. Unlike its more robust cousins, it features a milder spice blend, allowing the inherent tastes of chicken, fluffy potatoes, and aromatic rice to take center stage. The dish is meticulously assembled with alternating layers, then slow-cooked over low heat to create a perfect harmony. Subtle floral notes from rosewater and kewra water add a touch of elegance, making this biryani a delightful and fragrant experience.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg Chicken (bone-in pieces)
  • 3 cups Basmati rice
  • 3-4 medium size potatoes
  • 3-4 eggs
  • Yogurt - 1.5 cups
  • Cardamom, cloves, bay leaves (whole spices)
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced and fried golden brown (birista)
  • Ginger-garlic paste - 2 tbsp each
  • Red chili powder
  • turmeric powder
  • Saffron strands soaked in warm milk (saffron milk)
  • Kewra water (optional)
  • Biryani masala
  • Salt - to taste

Instructions

  1. Marinate the Chicken: Mix yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, biryani masala, and salt with chicken pieces. Marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Cook the Potatoes: Fry potatoes in oil with turmeric and red chili powder. Set aside.
  3. Hard-boil the Eggs: Bring a pot of water to a boil, add eggs gently and cook for 10-12 minutes. Peel and halve them carefully.
  4. Cook the Rice: Soak basmati rice for 30 minutes. Boil water with whole spices and salt. Add rice and cook until 80% done. Drain and spread on a tray.
  5. Cook the Chicken: Heat oil in a pan. Fry half the birista onions. Add marinated chicken and cook until browned. Add a little water and simmer for 20 minutes, or until chicken is tender.
  6. Assemble the Biryani: In a heavy-bottomed pot, layer half the cooked rice, followed by half the chicken pieces, fried potatoes, remaining birista onions, and half the halved eggs. Repeat with remaining rice, chicken, and eggs. Drizzle with saffron milk, kewra water (if using), and melted ghee.
  7. Seal and Cook: Seal the pot tightly with dough or foil. Cook over low heat for 20-25 minutes, or until heated through.
  8. Garnish and Serve: Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and enjoy your Kolkata-style chicken biryani with egg!
